## Design Context

### Users

**Who they are:**
- Maimai DX players of all skill levels - from casual to competitive
- Multi-regional audience (Japan, International, China)
- Mobile-first users (iOS app via WKWebView) and web users
- Multi-language speakers (English, Japanese, Chinese Simplified/Traditional)

**Their context:**
- Checking song data, difficulty ratings, and chart details
- Calculating rating scores and tracking progress
- Browsing community tags, comments, and aliases
- Using the app at arcades, at home, or on-the-go
- Need quick access to information in short sessions

**The job to be done:**
- Find specific songs/charts quickly
- Understand rating calculations
- Track personal progress and goals
- Discover community insights about songs

### Brand Personality

**Voice & Tone:**
- Playful and fun - reflecting the arcade rhythm game energy
- Approachable and welcoming for all skill levels
- Efficient and clear for power users
- Community-oriented and collaborative

**Three-word personality:**
Playful · Polished · Inclusive

**Emotional goals:**
- Delight users with smooth interactions and satisfying micro-animations
- Build confidence by making complex rating calculations clear
- Create community connection through shared game knowledge
- Maintain energy and excitement of the arcade experience

### Aesthetic Direction

**Visual tone:**
A balanced fusion of "Refined Minimalism" and "Arcade Maximalism":
- Clean, uncluttered layouts that prioritize content
- Strategic use of vibrant accent colors drawn from maimai's visual identity
- Playful touches that evoke arcade energy without overwhelming
- Version-themed accents that celebrate the game's evolving aesthetic

**Theme:**
Light mode primary (matches current implementation and arcade brightness)

**Version-themed accents:**
- Dynamic accent colors that shift with selected maimai version:
  - FESTiVAL+: Soft lavender (#c8a8f9)
  - BUDDiES/BUDDiES+: Warm gold (#FAAE29)
  - PRiSM/PRiSM+: Deep violet (#6368C7)
  - CiRCLE/CiRCLE+: Vivid pink (#FF4799)
- Version-specific backgrounds that set the mood
- Consistent neutral base with colorful accents

**Anti-references (what to avoid):**
- Generic gaming site templates
- Cluttered, information-dense layouts
- Overused purple gradient tech startup aesthetic
- Mobile game UI patterns (excessive badges, popups, gem counters)
- Boring system fonts (Arial, Inter, Roboto)

### Design Principles

1. **Celebrate the Game, Serve the User**
   Let maimai's vibrant art and music be the hero. The UI should frame the content, not compete with it. Use version themes to connect emotionally without cluttering.

2. **Energy Through Restraint**
   Create arcade energy through selective bold moments (page transitions, accent colors, playful micro-interactions) rather than visual noise. Every element earns its place.

3. **Mobile-First, Desktop-Refined**
   Design for touch-first mobile use where most users interact, then scale up gracefully for desktop. Optimize for one-handed use at arcades.

4. **Typography with Personality**
   Continue using Torus (rounded, playful) for body text. Pair with distinctive display fonts for headers that capture arcade/digital energy without being generic.

5. **Accessibility as Foundation**
   WCAG AA compliance is non-negotiable. High contrast text, clear focus states, reduced motion support, and screen reader friendly layouts. Beauty serves function.

6. **Cultural Inclusivity**
   Design accommodates 4 languages gracefully. Layouts must handle varying text lengths (Japanese compactness vs. English verbosity). Icons should transcend language.

### Technical Context

**Stack:**
- React 19 + Vite
- UnoCSS (Tailwind-compatible utility classes)
- Material-UI 5 + shadcn/ui primitives
- Framer Motion for animations
- Torus font family (custom webfont)

**Constraints:**
- Must work in WKWebView for iOS app
- Offline-capable with sql.js
- View transitions already implemented
- Safe area insets for notched devices
